Frequently Asked Questions

What is AAC format?

AAC (Advanced Audio Coding) is a modern audio codec that delivers significantly better sound quality than MP3 at the same or lower bitrate. It is the standard format for Apple Music, YouTube, and many streaming platforms.

Is AAC better than MP3?

Yes, AAC generally provides better sound quality than MP3 at equivalent bitrates, especially at lower bitrates (128 kbps and below). AAC is also the successor to MP3 and is supported by virtually all modern devices.

Which devices support AAC?

AAC is natively supported by all Apple devices, Android phones, modern browsers, PlayStation, Xbox, and most media players. It is one of the most widely compatible audio formats available.

Will there be quality loss when converting?

Since MP3 is already a lossy format, some minimal quality loss may occur during re-encoding. We recommend using 192 kbps AAC for optimal quality when converting from 128 kbps MP3.
